   Summary:

After becoming the Spider-Man of his universe as a teenager, Miles Morales must team up with five other versions' spider-powered people to stop a threat to all realities.

The visionaries behind The Lego Movie and 21 Jump Street, Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, lend their special talents to a new take on a different Spider-Man universe featuring a ground-breaking, first-of-its-kind visual style. Introduced in "Spider-Man(TM): Into the Spider-Verse" is Miles Morales (Shameik Moore), a teenage Brooklyn kid, and the infinite possibilities of the Spider-Verse, where multiple people can wear the mask.

Miles Morales is finding it difficult to fit in at his new school, where he recently started. After that, he gets bitten by a radioactive spider and gains abilities akin to Spider-Man. He instinctively goes to Spider-Man for guidance because he has no idea how to use or control his powers.

Reluctantly enrolling in an upscale boarding school in New York City, Miles Morales, a bright young man from Brooklyn, is trying to figure out who he is in the world while balancing academic and family obligations. While juggling his new duties, Miles stumbles onto a hidden new realm in an abandoned NYC subway tunnelâ€”only to be bitten by a cunning, radioactive spider that hails from another dimension. With mysterious powers that resemble those of the original Spider-Man (2002), Miles is surprised to learn that his life has gotten even more convoluted. Little does he know, however, that he is about to become embroiled in an awful inter-dimensional struggle. Despite his belief that not everyone is suited to be a hero, youthful and completely untrained Miles finds himself forced to grasp his unexpected skills while the multiverse's fate hangs in the balance. However, a hero does not always come out on top. Who is it that always tries? Is Miles able to take a risk and transform into Spider-Man?

   Sypnosis:

Teenage Spider-Man enthusiast Miles Morales (Shameik Moore) finds it difficult to live up to his parents' expectations, particularly those of his father, police officer Jefferson Davis (Brian Tyree Henry), who considers Spider-Man to be a threat. Miles resides with his mother Rio (Luna Lauren Velez) and father Jefferson. Jefferson feels that even though Spider-Man gets all the glory, police officers risk their lives every day without any special abilities. Miles recently started attending a new school, which he views as exclusive. The only other person he has made friends with at the new school is Gwanda, who just started there as well. Miles goes to see his uncle Aaron Davis (Mahershala Ali), a.k.a. The Prowler, in secret after school. Aaron takes Miles to an abandoned subway station so he can paint graffiti there. Miles gets accidentally bitten by a radioactive spider while he is there. The next day, he finds out that the bite has given him spider-like skills, such as the ability to turn invisible and release electric venom blasts. As Miles reads the comic books, he understands that he is Spider-Man. That there can be two spider-men astounds him.

Miles goes back to the station in search of the spider and inadvertently finds a particle accelerator constructed by Wilson Fisk (Liev Schreiber), also known as The Kingpin, who wants to access parallel universes in order to find other versions of his son and wife who perished in an automobile accident. Fighting Prowler (his own uncle Aaron) and Green Goblin (Jorma Taccone), two of Fisk's goons, Spider-Man (Chris Pine) attempts to disable it. Miles is prevented from falling into the particle accelerator by Spider-Man.Multiple dimensions become accessible as Fisk turns on the particle accelerator. Goblin forces Spider-Man into the particle accelerator's beams. An explosion that kills Green Goblin during the combat leaves Spider-Man with serious injuries. Spider-Man gives Miles, the guy he found earlier, a USB drive to disable the accelerator and tells him not to turn it on again because it could destroy the city. Miles then witnesses Spider-Man being killed by Fisk in terror.Spider-Man informs Fisk that he is aware of his plans and that they will fail because they are no longer there. Then word of Spider-Man's passing gets out over the entire city. In the meantime, a great number of objects and individuals from other universes infiltrate Miles's universe covertly thanks to the particle accelerator.

Miles uses his newly acquired skills to test out his Spider-Man suit, but in the process, he breaks the USB drive.
